## AEM (2024 - Present, Fulltime):
* Lead developer of fuel geospatial processing pipeline
* Purpose built pipeline to pull, cache, build, publish and distribute fire simulation inputs
* Designed & developed a system that combined [incremental builds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incremental_build_model), 
  [dependency digraphs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Directed_graph), arbitrary tile systems
  and [distributed computing](https://www.dask.org/) to produce fuel maps without wasted compute, in a customisable way and from unrelated
  data sources using on-demand AWS machines.
* Produced automated weekly fuel maps for significant coverage of Australia

## FireSight (2023 - 2024, Contract):
* Sole developer of the project
* Development of GUI tools to orchestrate the execution of Phoenix RapidFire, and combine results using Python
* Developed a QGIS plugin with algorithms to:
  * Analyse and export BOM weather for fire simulations
  * Analyse historical fire danger index ratings for extreme fire danger days
  * Analyse fire simulations and extract subdatasets: impact frequency, house loss probability, areas of interest, heatmaps and dangerous ignition zones
  * Determine frequency of historical days suitable for safe burns
* Developed a Google Earth Engine App to map live fuel moisture content using MODIS satellite imagery
